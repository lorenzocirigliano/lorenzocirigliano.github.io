About me

I am a Postdoc in Physics at the Department of Physics, Sapienza University of Rome, working in the group of Dr. Bernard Van Heck. My current research focuses on quantum communication networks, the robustness of networks under non-uniform correlated damage, and fundamental aspects of scale-free networks in the thermodynamic limit. I also have research interests in stochastic thermodynamics, nonlinear dynamical systems, open quantum systems, and complex systems with non-reciprocal interactions.

Born in Chiaromonte, Italy, on October 10, 1996, I completed my PhD in Physics on "Extended-range percolation and complex networks" under the supervision of Dr. Claudio Castellano. I hold a Doctor Europaeus title and have been an active researcher in statistical physics and complex systems.

Research Interests

  • Quantum communication networks: Investigating fundamental properties and optimization of quantum networks
  • Network robustness: Studying the resilience of networks under non-uniform correlated damage
  • Scale-free networks: Examining fundamental aspects in the thermodynamic limit
  • Extended-range percolation: Developing theoretical frameworks for percolation beyond nearest-neighbor interactions
  • Stochastic thermodynamics: Exploring non-equilibrium statistical mechanics
  • Nonlinear dynamical systems: Analysis of complex dynamical behavior
  • Open quantum systems: Theoretical investigations of quantum systems in contact with environments
  • Complex systems: Non-reciprocal interactions and emergent phenomena
